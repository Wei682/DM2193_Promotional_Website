Link between romanticism and modernity

Claude Debussy was a French composer. He is sometimes seen as the one who started the groundbreaking "Impressionist" style in music, though he rejected this term. Debussy's compositions defy conventional structures, instead, embracing ethereal harmonies and vivid imagery. Some of his famous works are Clair de Lune, Rêverie, and La Mer.


"I love music passionately. And because I love it I try to free it from barren traditions that stifle it." -- Claude Debussy
